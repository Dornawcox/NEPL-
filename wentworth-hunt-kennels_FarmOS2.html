Etiquette, Attire & Safety
Foxhunting customs · Field protocols · Learn more at wentworthhunt.org ↗
Formal Season Attire

Staff & Masters: Red (scarlet) coats, white breeches, black top hats or helmets

Members in Colors: By invitation of Masters — scarlet or black coat with hunt colors on collar

Members without Colors: Black or dark navy coat, white or canary breeches, black or brown boots

Ratcatcher (Informal): Tweed jacket or ratcatcher coat, beige or rust breeches, stock tie or choker

Helmets: Certified safety helmet always required — ASTM/SEI, SNELL, or EN1384

Stock Ties: Available at All Terrain Ties ↗

Hound & Kennel Etiquette

✦ Never speak to or feed hounds without permission of Huntsman or Kennelman

✦ Never enter kennel runs without Chuck Cox (Kennelman) present

✦ Hound walkers must attend safety briefing before first walk

✦ Report any injury, illness, or abnormal behavior immediately to Kennelman

✦ Do not approach bitches in season — runs are clearly marked

✦ Dogs must be kept separate from hounds at all Tuckaway Farm activities

Field Etiquette

✦ Follow the Field Master at all times — never overtake

✦ Never ride between hounds and a fox; never head a fox

✦ Give way to hounds at all times — they have absolute right of way

✦ Maintain safe distance from the horse ahead — minimum one horse length

✦ Shout "Ware hole!" or "Ware wire!" to warn riders behind you

✦ Close all gates — even if you must dismount

✦ Trot on verges, not road surfaces when possible

✦ Silence when hounds are working — no mobile phones

✦ All road crossings managed by ground crew — await signal

✦ Guests cap at the meet or pay Secretary at least 24 hrs prior

Tuckaway Farm Territory

Home of WWH Kennels — Lee, NH

250+ acres along the Oyster River · Organic farm (vegetables, fruits, hay, blueberries, grains, livestock & poultry)

What to expect: Rolling fields and wooded trails, stream crossings, pond, polo field, many jumps of all sizes (all gappable). No road crossings.

Also hunts: Powder Major's Farm (adjacent) — fields, woods, Stirrup Cup at the 1723 farmhouse beside the historic cemetery of Revolutionary War patriot John "Powder Major" Demeritt.
